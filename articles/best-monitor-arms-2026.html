01 // Why Every Desk Needs a Monitor Arm

The stock monitor stand consumes 80-120 square inches of prime real estate in front of you — space that could hold a notepad, a dock, or nothing at all (negative space is a battlestation aesthetic, not a luxury). A monitor arm frees that surface, enables infinite height and tilt beyond the built-in range, and rotates to portrait for coding or document review.

For standing desk users, arms are non-negotiable. When your sit-stand desk transitions from sitting to standing height, a fixed stand forces you to look down — defeating the ergonomic purpose. A gas-spring arm moves with the desk, keeping your eyeline locked at the top third of the screen.

06 // Clamp vs Grommet: How to Mount Without Destroying Your Desk

A C-clamp grips the desk edge — fast to install, no drilling, but needs at least 1.5 inches of overhang. A grommet mount inserts through a cable hole and bolts underneath — more secure for heavy arms, works against walls, but requires a grommet hole.

Mount Type Installation Desk Compatibility Stability Best Arm That Includes It
C-Clamp 5-minute tool-free install Desks with edge overhang ≥ 1.5" Good for arms under 20 lbs HUANUO Dual
Grommet Requires grommet hole or drill Any desk with a cable pass-through Superior for heavy ultrawide arms Amazon Basics / Ergotron LX
Freestanding Base Zero desk modification Glass, thin, or wall-flush desks Good if base is weighted VIVO Quad (optional base)
Pro Tip: Most C-clamps max out at 3.5 inches of desk thickness. Measure before buying — some premium arms like the Ergotron LX offer extended clamp kits sold separately. And don't neglect your chair setup — monitor height only matters relative to your seated eye level.
